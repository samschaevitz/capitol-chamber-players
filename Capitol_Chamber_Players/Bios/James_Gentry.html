James Gentry

 


JAMES GENTRY, baritone, has sung opera in the Central California area for thirty years. He has performed leading roles in many operas - such as, Madame Butterfly, Merry Widow, Der Freischutz, Die Fledermaus, La Boheme, and Amahl. He received rave reviews for his performance of Baron Scarpia in Puccini’s Tosca. He has also performed as soloist with many of the area’s symphonies and choruses and enjoys singing cantatas, oratorios and recitals. His performance of Mendelssohn’s was called “awe-inspiring” by critics. James is employed by the State of California and spent 28 years in the military - the last 15 in the Army Reserve Band playing French horn, bagpipes and as a vocalist.
