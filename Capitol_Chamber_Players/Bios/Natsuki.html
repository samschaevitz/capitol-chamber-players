Natsuki Fukasawa

 

Natsuki Fukasawa, pianist, is a graduate of the Juilliard School of Music and the Prague Academy of Music and recently earned her doctorate from the University of Maryland.  She began early studies with her mother and since then has studied with pianists Mark Richman, Martin Canin, Fenrenc Rados, Anne Koscielny, Jan Panenka, and Fuumiko Ishikawa.  She is a recipient of a Fulbright Scholarship.  Her career has taken her around the world as both soloist and chamber musician.  As a founding member of the award-winning Jalina Trio, Ms. Fukaswa has won many accolades and international prizes, including the 2004 Best Chamber Music Recording of the Year from the Danish Music Awards and a January 2006 rave review in Fanfare magazine.  The reviewer wrote of the trio’s recording of the Mendelssohn and Brahms piano trios: “Never—and I mean never—have I heard either of these works played with such expressive nuance and exquisite, heartfelt sensitivity.  When I am moved to tears of joy, I know I am hearing what music can and should be.” 

            Ms. Fukasawa collaborates regularly with many fine chamber musicians in Northern California and appears frequently in concerts.  Her recent local appearances include concerto performances with the Sacramento Philharmonic Orchestra and the Camellia Symphony Orchestra.  During the 2008-2009 season she performed the Beethoven Fourth Concerto and the Triple Concerto as well as Dohnanyi’s Variations on a Nursery Song for Piano and Orchestra.  She serves as an artist faculty member at the Schlern International Music Festival in Italy, coaches for the California Capital Chamber Music Workshop, and is the Associate Director of the Sacramento Youth Symphony Advanced Chamber Music Workshop.  She has also served as a visiting professor of piano at California State University, Sacramento, and St. Mary’s College of California in Moraga, and is a devoted teacher for her private studio.
